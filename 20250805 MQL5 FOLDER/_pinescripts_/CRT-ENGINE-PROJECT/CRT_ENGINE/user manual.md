## **User Manual: Institutional CRT Engine v3.0**

Welcome to the Institutional CRT Engine! This isn't just an indicator; it's a complete framework designed to help you identify and act on high-probability trading setups based on the Candle Range Theory.

The goal of this tool is to provide **CLARITY**. It follows a strict, top-down analysis, just like a professional trader, so you can stop guessing and start following a clear, repeatable process.

### **Section 1: The Core Idea - What the Indicator is Doing**

Before using the indicator, understand its core job. It's following a strict, 4-step process for you automatically:

1.  **Sets the Stage:** It identifies the single most important **timed candle range** for the session (e.g., the 8 AM NY hourly candle). This is our "playing field."
2.  **Waits for Manipulation:** It then patiently waits for the market to perform a **liquidity purge**, or "Turtle Soup," by sweeping above the high or below the low of that playing field.
3.  **Waits for Confirmation:** After the sweep, it looks for the most important confirmation signal: a **Market Structure Shift (MSS)** on the 15-minute timeframe. This proves institutions are likely stepping in.
4.  **Finds the Entry Zone:** After the MSS, it finds the **Fair Value Gap (FVG)** created by the strong move and alerts you to a potential entry when price pulls back to that zone.

Your job is simply to follow these clear signals and manage your trade.

### **Section 2: Setting Up the Indicator (Your 2-Minute Pre-Market Checklist)**

You should attach this indicator to a **15-Minute (M15) chart** for the best experience.

Before you start your trading session, you only need to configure a few key settings:

**Step 1: Open the Indicator Settings (`⚙️` icon).**

**Step 2: Go to the "CRT Core Settings" Group.**
*   **CRT Model:** Choose the session you are trading. **For US markets like Nasdaq or S&P 500, always choose `9 AM NY`.**
*   **Entry Logic:** Always leave this on **`CONFIRMATION_MSS`**. This is the safest and most reliable setting. `Aggressive Turtle Soup` is for highly advanced users and should not be used when you are starting out.

**Step 3: Go to the "Advanced Contextual Filters" Group.**
*   This is the most important setting for a new trader.
*   **"Require HTF Key Level Confluence": ✅ Make sure this is checked ON.** This is your most powerful filter. It will prevent the indicator from showing you signals in choppy, low-quality conditions and will force it to only show you setups that are occurring at significant higher-timeframe levels.

**Step 4: Go to the "Operational Mode" Group.**
*   **Operational Mode:** Start with **`SIGNALS_ONLY`**. This means the indicator will give you clear visual and sound alerts for setups, but **YOU** have the final say and must place the trade manually. Once you are consistently profitable with this method in a demo account, you can explore `FULLY_AUTOMATED`.

**That's it! Your indicator is now set up to hunt for A+ quality setups.**

### **Section 3: How to Interpret and Trade a Signal**

Your trading day is simple. You wait for the indicator to do the hard work and provide an alert.

#### **Example of a BULLISH (BUY) Signal:**

1.  **The Setup:** Throughout the day, the indicator will display the High and Low of the 8 AM hourly candle as two grey lines. This is your CRT range.
2.  **The Manipulation:** Price will trade **BELOW** the CRT Low line.
3.  **The Confirmation:** Price will then rally strongly, breaking the last swing high on your M15 chart.
4.  **The Alert:** An alert will fire and a **Green UP Arrow `▲`** will appear on your chart. The label will say **"BUY"**.

**What To Do:**

*   **Your Entry:** The indicator has automatically identified a Fair Value Gap (the "entry zone"). The best practice is to place a **limit buy order** at the top of that FVG zone.
*   **Your Stop Loss:** Place your stop loss **just below the low point of the manipulation sweep** (the lowest point price reached when it went below the CRT Low).
*   **Your Take Profit:** The indicator's purpose is to find high-probability setups that deliver a quick move. A good primary target is a **1:1 or 1:2 Risk-to-Reward ratio.**

#### **Example of a BEARISH (SELL) Signal:**

1.  **The Setup:** The indicator displays the 8 AM CRT range.
2.  **The Manipulation:** Price will trade **ABOVE** the CRT High line.
3.  **The Confirmation:** Price will then fall sharply, breaking the last swing low on your M15 chart.
4.  **The Alert:** An alert will fire and a **Red DOWN Arrow `▼`** will appear. The label will say **"SELL"**.

**What To Do:**

*   **Your Entry:** Place a **limit sell order** at the bottom of the identified Fair Value Gap.
*   **Your Stop Loss:** Place your stop loss **just above the high point of the manipulation sweep.**
*   **Your Take Profit:** Target a **1:1 or 1:2 Risk-to-Reward ratio.**
