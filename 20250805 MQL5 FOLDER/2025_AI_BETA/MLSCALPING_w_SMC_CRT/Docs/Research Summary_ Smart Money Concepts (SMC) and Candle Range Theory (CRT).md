# Research Summary: Smart Money Concepts (SMC) and Candle Range Theory (CRT)

## Smart Money Concepts (SMC)

SMC is a trading framework that focuses on understanding the actions and motives of large institutional market participants, often referred to as "smart money." The core idea is to align trading strategies with these influential entities by analyzing variables such as supply, demand dynamics, and market structure.

Key components of SMC include:

*   **Order Blocks:** Concentrated areas of limit orders awaiting execution, identified by analyzing past price movements for significant shifts. These act as pivotal points influencing future market direction.
*   **Fair Value Gap:** An imbalance in the market where price departs from a specific level with limited trading activity, resulting in one-directional movement. It represents a discrepancy that may indicate a potential trading opportunity.
*   **Liquidity:** Price levels where orders accumulate, making an asset class "liquid." This can manifest as highs, lows, or trend line liquidity. SMC traders aim to capitalize on market movements driven by smart money seeking liquidity.
*   **Break of Structure (BOS):** A fundamental element of SMC market analysis where price surpasses a previous high (in an uptrend) or drops below a previous low (in a downtrend), indicating a continuation of the trend.
*   **Change of Character (ChoCH):** Signals a potential reversal in market direction, often occurring when price breaks a previous low in an uptrend or a previous high in a downtrend.

SMC is often compared to Wyckoff's Theory, which also focuses on understanding the behavior of "smart money" through price and volume analysis across accumulation, markup, distribution, and markdown phases.

## Candle Range Theory (CRT)

CRT is a trading concept that analyzes price movement within a candlestick to understand market behavior, often derived from ICT trading concepts like Liquidity Sweep, Power of 3, and Session High/Low Liquidity.

Key aspects of CRT:

*   **Candle Range:** Every candlestick on a higher timeframe represents a range on a lower timeframe. The high of a candlestick is the CRT-High, and the low is the CRT-Low.
*   **Core Principle:** CRT is based on the liquidity raid of the previous candle's range, followed by a run to the next liquidity level. For example, if price grabs the liquidity of the previous candle's low, it is expected to run towards the high of that candle's range.
*   **Bullish CRT Model:** At a key support level, mark the high and low of the candlestick. Wait for the next candlestick to grab the low of the previous candle and close above it. Then, wait for another candlestick to close above the high of the candlestick that raided the low, or look for an ICT Market Structure Shift on a lower timeframe for a buy entry.
*   **Bearish CRT Model:** At a key resistance level, mark the high and low of the candlestick. Wait for the next candlestick to grab the high of the previous candle and close below it. Then, wait for another candlestick to close below the low of the candlestick that took out the high, or look for an ICT Market Structure Shift on a lower timeframe for a sell entry.
*   **High Probability Setups:** CRT setups are often found during ICT Kill Zones or ICT Sessions Raid.
*   **Relationship with ICT Power of 3:** CRT is essentially an application of ICT Power of 3, which states that price will accumulate, then manipulate by taking short-term liquidity, and then distribute by moving in the opposite direction. CRT-High/Low can also be the ICT previous day high/low or previous session high/low.
