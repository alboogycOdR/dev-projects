## The 714 Method Trading Strategy: Comprehensive Consolidated Summary

The "714 method" is presented as a simple, time-based scalping strategy focusing on exploiting anticipated price movements and potential liquidity manipulation around specific key times, particularly in the UTC+2 time zone (also referred to as SAST - South African Standard Time). It prioritizes price action analysis over numerous indicators. The strategy's name, "714," likely relates to trading around 7 AM and 1 PM session times ("71") with the aim of achieving profit quickly, perhaps within approximately four 5-minute candlesticks ("4") after entry.

### Key Components and Setup:

*   **Scalping Strategy:** This method is designed for quick trades with short holding periods, emphasizing getting in and out of the market efficiently. **(Confirmed by video)**
*   **Primary Time Focus: 1 PM UTC+2 (13:00 UTC+2 / 14h00 SAST):** This time is the core anchor for the strategy. It's identified as a significant point for potential market shifts, liquidity grabs, or the start of the main move for the session. **(Confirmed by video)**
*   **Secondary Time Focus: 7 AM UTC+2 (07h00 SAST):** Also noted as an important "opening hour" to observe initial price behavior. **(Mentioned in images)**
*   **Trading Platform Time Zone:** It is absolutely critical to set your trading platform's time zone to **UTC+2** (SAST). **(Strongly emphasized in markdown 2 and video)** The video clarifies that if your platform (like MetaTrader 5, which is often GMT+0) is different, you must account for the difference (GMT+0 is 2 hours behind UTC+2). So, 1 PM UTC+2 (13:00) would be 11 AM GMT+0. **(Clarified and emphasized in video)**
*   **Timeframe: 5-Minute (M5):** The strategy is specifically built for the M5 timeframe to allow for the detailed observation needed for scalping. **(Confirmed by markdown 2 and video)**
*   **Visual Anchor: Vertical Line at 1 PM UTC+2:** Draw a vertical line at precisely 1:00 PM (13:00) on your UTC+2 (SAST) chart daily. This line is your primary reference point for observation. **(Confirmed by markdown 2 and video)** Vertical lines may also be used for other key times like 2 PM UTC+2 (14:00 UTC+2). **(Shown in video example)**

### Core Trading Logic:

The strategy's core principle revolves around observing the initial market movement immediately after the 1 PM UTC+2 mark and often looking for opportunities to trade in the **opposite direction** of this initial move, expecting a reversal driven by market makers targeting liquidity.

1.  **Observe Price from 1 PM UTC+2 Onwards:** Begin watching how price behaves immediately after the 1 PM vertical line. **(Confirmed by markdown 2 and video)** Observe for approximately an hour (until 2 PM UTC+2) to gauge the initial direction. **(Mentioned in video)**
2.  **Trade Against the Initial Post-1 PM Trend (Liquidity Manipulation):** This is a key "golden rule" of the strategy. **(Strongly emphasized in markdown 2 and video)**
    *   If price moves **up** and is bullish after 1 PM UTC+2, look for **selling opportunities**. **(Confirmed by markdown 2 and video)**
    *   If price moves **down** and is bearish after 1 PM UTC+2, look for **buying opportunities**. **(Confirmed by markdown 2 and video)**
    *   *Reasoning:* The strategy suggests this initial move may be a "fake" move engineered by market makers to trap retail traders before the price moves in the opposite direction to collect liquidity. **(Explained in markdown 2 and video)**
