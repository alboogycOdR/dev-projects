# Product-ready spec — Liquidity Sweep + Market Structure Shift

---

## 1 — Strategy summary (single sentence)

Detect stop-hunts (liquidity sweeps) beyond recent swing extremes, wait for a fast rejection and a lower-timeframe structure flip, then enter opposite the sweep with disciplined stop and sized position.

---

## 2 — Key definitions (unambiguous)

* **Swing High / Swing Low**: highest high / lowest low over a configurable base-window (e.g., 24h or last N candles on 1H/4H).
* **Liquidity Zone**: region just above swing high or below swing low where clustered stops likely exist. Represent as `[level - margin, level + margin]`.
* **Sweep**: price candle/wick that moves beyond the liquidity zone by at least `sweep_buffer_pct` (e.g., 0.1–0.5%) within a short timeframe.
* **Rejection**: price returns back inside the prior range within `rejection_time_ms` or `rejection_candles`.
* **Structure Shift (confirmation)**: lower-timeframe break of the last minor structure in the opposite direction (e.g., break of a minor swing high/low or higher-low / lower-high pattern).

---

## 3 — Detection algorithm (strict, step-by-step)

1. **Base timeframe** (e.g., 1H or 4H): compute `swing_high` and `swing_low` using last `M` candles (configurable).
2. Define `high_zone = [swing_high, swing_high * (1 + zone_margin_pct)]` and `low_zone = [swing_low * (1 - zone_margin_pct), swing_low]`.

   * Defaults: `zone_margin_pct = 0.0015 (0.15%)` (tune per symbol).
3. **Detect sweep** on confirm timeframe (e.g., 1m or 5m): if any wick/high > `high_zone[1]` then mark `high_sweep_time`; likewise low < `low_zone[0]` for `low_sweep_time`.

   * Optional volume filter: require volume spike `> vol_mean * vol_multiplier` to prioritize genuine hunts.
4. **Rejection check**: within `rejection_time_ms` (e.g., 3,000–30,000 ms) or `rejection_candles` (e.g., 1–3 candles), price must close back inside the prior range: `close < swing_high` (after high sweep) or `close > swing_low` (after low sweep).
5. **Structure confirmation** on confirm TF: require a structure break opposite the sweep. Examples:

   * For high sweep → short: confirm a lower-timeframe close below the previous local minor low OR a lower-low sequence.
   * For low sweep → long: confirm a close above the previous minor high OR a higher-high sequence.
6. **Final entry trigger**: after rejection + confirmation, place entry (market or limit). Include optional buffered entry price to improve fills.

---

## 4 — Parameter defaults (starting point; must be tunable)

* Base TF for swings: `1h` or `4h` (user choice).
* Confirm TF: `1m` or `5m`.
* Swing lookback: `24` bars (1-day for 1h) or `30` bars (configurable).
* `zone_margin_pct`: `0.15%` (0.0015).
* `sweep_buffer_pct`: `0.05% - 0.5%` (0.0005–0.005). Start `0.15%`.
* `rejection_time_ms`: `3000` (3s) to `30000` (30s) — default `5000`.
* `rejection_candles`: `1-3` on confirm TF — default `2`.
* `volume_multiplier`: `2.0` (sweep volume must be > 2× recent mean volume) — optional.
* Risk per trade: `1%` of account (default) or less.
* Max leverage: user-specified; recommended `<=5x` for beginners.

---

## 5 — Order execution & sizing (robust)

1. **Pre-checks**

   * Check account futures wallet balance and isolated/cross margin mode.
   * Validate symbol trading rules (`minNotional`, `stepSize`, lot precision`) via `GET /fapi/v1/exchangeInfo\`.
   * Check open positions for same symbol (honor one-position-policy or hedge mode).
2. **Sizing**

   * `risk_amount = account_balance * risk_pct`.
   * `stop_distance = abs(entry_price - stop_price)` in USDT terms (use mark price).
   * `contracts = risk_amount / stop_distance` (for USDT-M perpetuals, compute base qty properly with step size).
   * Enforce `minNotional` and step size rounding.
3. **Entry**

   * Prefer **market order** for immediate entry in high-prob flows OR **limit** near recent candle close if liquidity sufficient to improve price. For webhooks from TV: prefer market with `quoteOrderQty`.
4. **Stop-loss**

   * Place STOP\_MARKET reduce-only order at `stop_price` just beyond sweep extreme (e.g., `sweep_extreme + stop_buffer_pct`).
   * Use markPrice-based stop where supported to avoid unfair liquidations.
5. **Take profit**

   * Primary TP at nearest liquidity pool (previous swing/opposite structure) OR R\:R 1:2 minimum.
   * Use LIMIT reduce-only order. Consider multi-TakeProfit legs (partial close).
6. **Position management**

   * Optionally trail stop behind confirmed structure.
   * If funding time/funding cost > threshold, consider early exit.
7. **Post-trade reconciliation**

   * Confirm fills using user-data stream (listenKey) or `GET /fapi/v1/order` and `GET /fapi/v2/positionRisk`.
   * If partial fills, manage remaining qty correctly (adjust stop/TP).

## 6 — Failure modes & mitigations

* **Network/API failures**: implement retries with exponential backoff and idempotency keys.
* **Partial fills**: detect and reissue stop/TP for partial qty.
* **Missed stop placement**: if stop creation fails, attempt immediate position close + alert.
* **Flash spike beyond stop then gap**: use reduce-only market close if STOP\_MARKET not executed.
* **Rate limits**: implement local rate limiter and monitor response headers; fallback to cached state if rate-limited.
* **Data divergence**: ensure consistent source of price (mark price vs last trade) across detection and stop triggers. Use markPrice for stop decisions.

## 7 — Testing & QA (rigorous)

1. **Unit tests** for detection logic (sweep detection, rejection, confirmation) with contrived candlestick sequences.
2. **Backtest** on tick-level or 1s data if available to simulate sweeps precisely (candles can hide quick wicks). Use VectorBT/Backtrader with Binance historical data.
3. **Paper trade** on Binance Futures Testnet for 2–4 weeks with production-like latency and sizes.
4. **Replay testing**: implement historical replay engine that replays tick/trade stream and verifies the bot’s decisions match expected trades.
5. **Edge-case tests**:

   * Multiple near-simultaneous sweeps.
   * Low-liquidity symbols where wicks are unreliable.
   * High volatility / news scenarios.
6. **Acceptance criteria**

   * Strategy produces positive expectancy on in-sample/backtest (configurable target).
   * Drawdown within acceptable bounds.
   * All automated stop placements succeed in >99% of testnet trades.
   * Alerts/notifications fire on error/stop failure.

* Execution latency exists in live; use market orders or slippage buffers in sizing/TP/SL.
* Funding rates, insurance, and liquidation mechanics can change expected PnL — include funding simulation in backtest.
