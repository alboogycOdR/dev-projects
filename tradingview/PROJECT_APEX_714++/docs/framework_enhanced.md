## The Enhanced 714 Method: An Integrated Wyckoff & ICT Approach

### **Part 1: The Core Philosophy (The "Why")**

This strategy operates on the principle that the market is not moved by retail traders, but by institutional "Smart Money." These large players must accumulate their positions without causing spikes in price and distribute them at a profit. This process leaves a specific footprint on the charts, which we aim to identify and follow.

Our philosophy is built on three pillars:

1.  **Market Structure (The Trend):** We use ICT principles to define the market's direction. Price moves in one of two states: **expansion** (trending) or **consolidation** (ranging). Our job is to trade with the primary trend.
2.  **Liquidity & The Wyckoff Cycle (The Fuel):** The original strategy's "M" and "W" patterns are simplified versions of the **Wyckoff Accumulation and Distribution Schematics**. These are blueprints for how institutional players build and unload positions. They engineer **liquidity** (pools of stop-loss orders above highs and below lows) to enter the market efficiently.
3.  **Time & Price Theory (The When & Where):** The market does not move uniformly throughout the 24-hour cycle. Specific sessions, or **"Killzones,"** see an injection of volatility where institutional manipulation is most likely to occur. The original's 7 AM, 1 PM, and 4 PM SAST times align perfectly with these high-volatility periods.

### **Part 2: The Trader's Toolkit (The "What")**

Before executing, you must be able to identify these key price action features on your M5 and M15 charts.

*   **Higher Timeframe (HTF) Bias:** The overall direction determined on the H4/Daily chart. You should only be looking for buy setups in a bullish market and sell setups in a bearish market.
*   **Key Terminology:**
    *   **Change of Character (CHoCH):** The *first* sign of a potential reversal. In an uptrend, it's the first time a swing low is broken. In a downtrend, it's when a swing high is broken. This signals a shift in momentum.
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** A confirmation of the trend. In an uptrend, it's a new Higher High. In a downtrend, it's a new Lower Low.
*   **Institutional Reference Points:**
    *   **Order Block (OB):** The last up-candle before a strong down-move (Bearish OB), or the last down-candle before a strong up-move (Bullish OB). This is an area where smart money has shown its hand.
    *   **Fair Value Gap (FVG) / Imbalance:** A three-candle pattern where there is an inefficient gap in price delivery. Price is often drawn back to these gaps to "rebalance" price action, making them high-probability entry zones.
*   **Liquidity:**
    *   **Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL):** Rests above previous highs. Smart money can trigger these buy-stops to sell their positions to.
    *   **Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL):** Rests below previous lows. Smart money can trigger these sell-stops to buy their positions from. The Asian session highs and lows are major pools of BSL and SSL.
*   **Execution Zones:**
    *   **Premium:** The top 50% of a trading range (use the Fibonacci tool from high to low). **We only look for SELLS in a premium market.**
    *   **Discount:** The bottom 50% of a trading range. **We only look for BUYS in a discount market.**

### **Part 3: The Complete Trading Plan (The "How")**

This is a step-by-step process. Do not skip any steps.

#### **Step 1: Pre-Market Analysis (Daily Prep)**

1.  **Define HTF Bias:** Go to the H4 chart. Is the market making Higher Highs and Higher Lows (Bullish) or Lower Lows and Lower Highs (Bearish)? This is your overall directional bias for the day. Write it down.
2.  **Identify Key Levels:** Mark out the Previous Day's High and Low, and the Previous Week's High and Low. These are major liquidity targets.

#### **Step 2: Session Analysis (The Narrative for the Day)**

1.  **Mark the Asian Range:** Mark the high and low of the Asian session (typically from 20:00 to 00:00 EST / 02:00 to 06:00 SAST). The liquidity resting above and below this range will be the probable target for the London session.
2.  **Formulate a Thesis:** Based on the HTF bias, anticipate which side of the Asian range will be "swept" or "raided" to induce traders into the wrong position.
    *   *Example:* If the H4 trend is Bullish, you might anticipate price will first dip *below* the Asian low to sweep the sell-side liquidity (a "stop hunt") before moving aggressively higher.

#### **Step 3: Execution - The A.M.D. Blueprint**

The core of this strategy is the **ICT Power of Three**, which aligns perfectly with Wyckoff:
*   **A - Accumulation:** The Asian Range, where orders are being accumulated.
*   **M - Manipulation:** The stop hunt or liquidity sweep that occurs during the **London Killzone (02:00-05:00 EST / 08:00-11:00 SAST)**. This is the false move.
*   **D - Distribution:** The real, high-volume move in the direction of the HTF bias.

**The Bullish Setup (Wyckoff Accumulation):**

1.  **Prerequisite:** H4 Bias is Bullish.
2.  **Manipulation:** During the London Killzone, wait for price to aggressively trade **below** a key low (e.g., the Asian session low or the previous day's low). This is the liquidity sweep, also known as a Wyckoff "Spring."
3.  **Confirmation (CHoCH):** After the sweep, watch the M5 chart. Wait for price to rally and break above a recent, clear swing high. This **Change of Character** is your confirmation that bulls are taking control.
4.  **Identify Entry Zone:** The up-move after the CHoCH will have created a **Bullish Order Block** or a **Fair Value Gap (FVG)**. This area is now in a **Discount**.
5.  **Place Entry:** Set a limit order to buy when price returns to mitigate the OB or fill 50% of the FVG.

**The Bearish Setup (Wyckoff Distribution):**

1.  **Prerequisite:** H4 Bias is Bearish.
2.  **Manipulation:** During the London Killzone, wait for price to aggressively trade **above** a key high (e.g., the Asian session high). This liquidity sweep is known as a Wyckoff "Upthrust After Distribution" (UTAD).
3.  **Confirmation (CHoCH):** After the sweep, wait for price to fall and break below a recent swing low on the M5 chart. This is the bearish **Change of Character**.
4.  **Identify Entry Zone:** The down-move after the CHoCH will create a **Bearish Order Block** or a **Fair Value Gap (FVG)**. This area is now in a **Premium**.
5.  **Place Entry:** Set a limit order to sell when price rallies back to the OB or FVG.

### **Part 4: Risk and Trade Management (THE MOST IMPORTANT PART)**

A strategy is useless without impeccable risk management. These are not suggestions; they are rules.

1.  **The 1% Rule:** Never risk more than 1% of your total account equity on a single trade.
2.  **Stop Loss Placement:**
    *   **For Buy Trades:** Your stop loss MUST be placed a few pips below the absolute low of the manipulation move (the wick of the "Spring").
    *   **For Sell Trades:** Your stop loss MUST be placed a few pips above the absolute high of the manipulation move (the wick of the "UTAD").
3.  **Position Sizing (CRITICAL):** Use a position size calculator for every trade. The formula is:
    `Position Size = (Account Equity * Risk %) / (Stop Loss in Pips * Pip Value)`
4.  **Profit Targets (R:R Ratio):**
    *   **TP1:** Aim for a 1:2 Risk-to-Reward Ratio. When price hits this level, **close 50%-80% of your position and move your stop loss to your entry point (break-even)**.
    *   **TP2:** Your final target should be a significant HTF liquidity level, such as the opposite side of the Asian range or the previous day's high/low.
