Document Version: 3.1
Last Updated: September 1, 2025

1. Overview
The Apex Protocol v3.0 is an institutional-grade, multi-strategy TradingView indicator built on a pure Smart Money Concepts (SMC) foundation. It is designed not just to find signals, but to operate as a complete, state-based trading framework that hunts for specific, user-defined trade models within designated market sessions (Killzones).

At its core, the system separates a robust SMC Analysis Engine from its Strategy Logic. The engine's sole responsibility is to analyze raw price action and identify a library of fundamental SMC "Points of Interest" (POIs). This data is then fed into selectable strategy modules that contain the step-by-step logic for high-probability setups.

2. Key Features
State-Based Logic Engine: The protocol operates on a clear four-state machine, providing an at-a-glance understanding of its current operation:

STANDBY: Outside of active Killzones.

HUNTING: Inside a Killzone, actively searching for the first event of a selected strategy.

CONFIRMING: One or more events have been met; waiting for final confirmation.

ARMED: All conditions for a strategy have been met. A single alert is fired and the trade is visualized.

Fully Customizable Killzones: Target specific market volatility with three user-defined sessions:

🇯🇵 Midnight Killzone (Defaults to 02:00-05:00 SAST / 00:00-03:00 UTC)

🇬🇧 London Killzone (Defaults to 08:00-11:00 UTC)

🇺🇸 New York Killzone (Defaults to 15:00-18:00 UTC)

Multiple, Selectable Strategy Modules: Assign a unique, high-probability trade model to each Killzone.

The Core SMC Model: A comprehensive 4-step validation model.

ICT Silver Bullet: A simplified 2-step model for rapid entry.

The Midnight Reversal: A specialized 2-step model designed to capture inducement/reversal plays.

Comprehensive SMC Analysis Suite: The engine detects a wide array of institutional concepts, including:

Market Structure: Validated Break of Structure (BOS) and Market Structure Shift (MSS / CHoCH).

Internal & External Structure: Differentiates between micro and macro structural shifts.

Liquidity: Asian Session High/Low, Previous Day High/Low (PDH/PDL).

Price Imbalances: Fair Value Gaps (FVG), Order Blocks (OB), and Inversion FVGs (IFVG).

Advanced Confluences: Change in State of Delivery (CISD) and SMT Divergence with a correlated asset (e.g., DXY).

Dynamic On-Chart Dashboard: A clean, professional UI panel provides a real-time summary of the Protocol Status, HTF Bias, active Killzone, and a live checklist that tracks the progress of the current strategy.

Explicit Trade Visualization: When a trade becomes ARMED, the system automatically draws the precise Entry Line, Stop Loss Line, and Take Profit Line on the chart, calculated based on your specified Risk-to-Reward ratio.

Precision Alert System: A single, definitive alert is fired only when a setup is fully confirmed. The alert message is dynamic, containing the Killzone, strategy name, direction, and exact price levels for Entry, SL, and TP.

3. Strategy Modules Explained
The Core SMC Model
A strict, four-step validation sequence for high-conviction setups.

Event 1: Manipulation Confirmed: A key liquidity level is swept OR an SMT Divergence occurs.

Event 2: Structure Break Confirmed: A clear MSS (reversal) or BOS (continuation) is printed.

Event 3: Return to HTF POI: Price retraces into a valid 5-Minute FVG or Order Block.

Event 4: LTF Entry Confirmation: An Inversion FVG forms on the Lower Timeframe, signaling rejection.

ICT Silver Bullet
A simplified model for faster entries during specific windows.

Event 1: Liquidity Sweep: A sweep of a recent session or swing high/low.

Event 2: Displacement FVG: A strong price move creates a clean Fair Value Gap, which serves as the entry.

The Midnight Reversal
A specialized model for capturing reversal plays after an inducement.

Event 1: Inducement Confirmed: A Change in State of Delivery (CISD) signal appears against the HTF bias, suggesting liquidity is being engineered.

Event 2: Reversal Confirmed: A subsequent CISD signal or an Internal Change of Character (I-CHoCH) appears in the direction of the HTF bias, confirming the reversal.

4. How to Use
Configure Timeframes: Set your desired Higher Timeframe (HTF) for bias and Lower Timeframe (LTF) for confirmation.

Select a Killzone: Go to the "Killzone Sessions" settings.

Assign a Strategy: Use the dropdown menu to assign one of the three strategy modules to that Killzone.

Monitor the Dashboard: As price enters the Killzone, the protocol will become HUNTING. Watch the on-chart checklist to follow the strategy's progress in real-time.

Wait for the Signal: Do nothing until the protocol status changes to ARMED. This will trigger an alert and draw the complete trade parameters on your chart for review.
