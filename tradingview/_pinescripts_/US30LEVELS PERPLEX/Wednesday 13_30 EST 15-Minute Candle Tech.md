The **Wednesday 13:30 EST 15-Minute Candle Technique** is a popular method among intraday gold and Forex traders for establishing key support and resistance levels, especially in highly liquid instruments like gold. Here’s a detailed explanation of the technique and why it’s used:

## What Is the Wednesday 13:30 EST 15-Minute Candle Technique?

- **Every Wednesday at 13:30 EST**, major economic data is often released (such as U.S. CPI, PPI, Fed announcements, etc.).
- At this time, the market typically experiences a **surge in volatility and trading volume**.
- By marking both the **high and low of the 13:30–13:45 EST 15-minute candle** on your chart, you establish reference price levels that are likely to draw significant market attention for the rest of the session—and sometimes throughout the rest of the week.


## Why Is This Candle Important?

- It frequently becomes the **range for price expansion** or a “trap for liquidity,” meaning institutional players may use this period to sweep stops or set up directional moves.
- The boundaries of this candle can act as **support and resistance** for subsequent price action. When price approaches these levels later, it often reacts sharply (e.g., fakeouts, breakouts, or reversals).
- Many times, **true breakouts** from this range signal the continuation of the move set in motion by economic data, while false breaks often precede reversals.


## How to Use the Technique

1. **On your chart, find Wednesday’s 13:30–13:45 EST candle** (adjust for your platform’s time zone, if needed).
2. **Mark the high and low** of this 15-minute candle with horizontal lines.
3. **Observe price action** around these levels for the rest of Wednesday and, sometimes, for the next few trading days:
    - If price returns to these levels, watch for reversals or continuation.
    - If price breaks out convincingly, look for entry opportunities in the breakout direction (with trend confirmation).
    - If price fakes out above/below and then reverses, this may signal a stop hunt or liquidity sweep.

## Practical Insights

- Entries and exits are often most effective when combined with confirmation from other tools (order blocks, fair value gaps, supply/demand zones, or candle pattern confirmation).
- Many traders use this technique alongside broader session and weekly cycle analysis, such as Quarterly Theory, to add an extra layer of precision and confluence.
- This method can be practiced and backtested easily—just check past Wednesdays and see how gold (or other assets) respects these key levels.
